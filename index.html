Oracle Lab

AI Prediction Market Forecasting

What is Oracle Lab?

Oracle Lab is an AI-powered forecasting system that predicts outcomes on Polymarket prediction markets. It uses large language models (Claude Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus) to analyze 23 active contracts spanning geopolitics, economics, politics, tech, sports, and entertainment.

The system runs autonomously on a cloud server, executing forecast cycles every few hours. Each cycle pulls live market prices, gathers recent news and intelligence, then uses a tiered LLM pipeline to generate probability estimates for each contract. Haiku handles initial triage, Sonnet performs deep analysis on the most interesting contracts, and Opus tackles the highest-complexity questions.

The core question we're investigating: can LLMs systematically outperform prediction market prices? Markets aggregate the wisdom of thousands of traders with real money at stake. Beating them consistently would demonstrate genuine analytical capability beyond pattern matching.

How It Works

1
Data Collection

Pull live prices from Polymarket for all 23 contracts. Gather fresh news via Perplexity and GDELT.

2
Triage (Haiku)

Claude Haiku quickly assesses all contracts, flagging those where our analysis diverges most from market consensus.

3
Deep Analysis (Sonnet/Opus)

Higher-tier models perform detailed reasoning with adversarial second passes on the most divergent contracts.

4
Calibration

Raw predictions are shrunk toward market prices to correct for systematic overconfidence, a known LLM limitation.

5
Scoring

As contracts resolve, predictions are scored using Brier scores and compared against market prices.

Why This Matters

Prediction markets are one of the hardest benchmarks for AI reasoning. Unlike trivia or standardized tests, they require synthesizing incomplete information, reasoning under uncertainty, understanding strategic incentives, and calibrating confidence. Academic research has shown that LLMs exhibit "systematic overconfidence across all models" and that "extended reasoning worsens rather than improves calibration." This project is an empirical test of whether careful system design can overcome these limitations.

This project is built by Pairie Koh, extending the earlier work of Professor Andrew Hall of Stanford University on AI-driven prediction market agents, documented in the Free Systems blog.
